About me

Roberto Fortich Mesa is an economist who works in the provision of advice to businesses on management issues, such as financial planning and budgeting, and marketing objectives and policies.Fortich has 15 years of experience in industry and academia, with a solid backgorund in the teaching of economics, where he holds a PhD(c) in economics. During his career, Fortich has tried to combine two valuable skills: data science and writing.

Roberto’s research interests are the funding of innovation, and the study of controlling shareholders. He is a former editor of the Economía y Región journal, as well as former Director of the Instituto de Estudios para el Desarrollo at Universidad Tecnológica de Bolívar, Colombia.

He has published nine peer-reviewed papers, book reviews, and over 55 op eds (they are archived at roberto’s blog). He is the main author of the online textbook “Teoría de la decisión con ciencia de datos” along with coauthors José Vergara and Julio Hurtado. The topic of a series of his blog posts on the coverage of the lab leak theory of SARS-CoV-2 origin were featured in the Australian News Media in 2021.

In regards to data analysis, Roberto works with the R programming language, in which he has expertise in the packages dplyr, ggplot2, and tidyr. He has developed projects in financial simulations with risk (Monte Carlo simulations with R), the teaching of data management in R, the teaching causal inference (static data panel, exact matching) and has a beginner knowledge of Python programming. A portfolio of projects is displayed in his Github account.
